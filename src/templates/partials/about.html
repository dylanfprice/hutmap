About Hutmap

Hutmap was built by two ragtag mountain enthusiasts as a service to the independent-minded outdoor community. Cabins, yurts, lean-tos, lookouts, and lodges! Platforms on stilts, camperized railroad cars, natural caves! If it provides covered shelter in the backcountry, it belongs on Hutmap. Keeping with the spirit of the map, only superbly-located, community-supported frontcountry accommodations are included. Expensive, guide-only operations are (grudgingly) included.

For the time being, Hutmap covers only the USA and Canada. If you know of huts we overlooked, or would like to help us expand into new territories - say by providing local knowledge or language translation - contact us!

Write us at info@hutmap.com. We would love to hear from you!

Ethan Welty / Chief Hut Wrangler

A late night at the computer mapping backcountry shelters in Ethan's home ranges of Washington and Colorado quickly turned into a much larger journey across the landscapes of North America - combing satellite images, topographic maps, and the web in a quest to satiate his ravenous appetite for huts. He currently lives in Boulder, Colorado where he geeks out on glaciers, photography, and maps for his PhD at the University of Colorado.

Dylan Price / Web Guru

Dylan and Ethan met through the Climbing Club at the University of Washington, where Dylan schooled in the art of web development. Once graduated, Dylan jumped on board to help share Ethan's hut database with the world. He currently lives out of his car as a climbing bum somewhere in the wilds of the western US, and generously reports back from coffee shops to work on the website.

Closing Remarks

Backcountry travel is a gift and a responsibility - please be safe, considerate of others (and their property), and cherish the natural environment. Where specified, hut locations are approximate and should not be relied upon for navigation. Images copied from hut agency websites are used without permission on hut profiles besides links to the page of origin, on the assumption that this classifies as Fair Use. We are sensitive to the rights of photographers, so please contact us if you disagree.

Our site makes use of the Google Maps API. By using this site, you are bound by both the Google Terms of Service and the Google Privacy Policy.
